How I learned Web Development

Throughout my learning journey, The Odin Project has been the main resource to learn web development, from the beginning setting me up to an actual development environment, showing me how the Internet actually works, and more beyond the technical aspect. What I appreciate about it is the particular method used in the curriculum, taking information from different sources, it encourages your own research which is vital in this field, my learning was solidified by their project-based approach.

While going through the curriculum I have made a vast amount of projects ranging from a landing page, a calculator, tic-tac-toe game, sign-up form, todo list, library app, dashboard, among others. This focus on projects gave me the confidence to create my own projects and a good taste of what it feels like being a developer. I discovered that creativity and problem-solving are the essential factor and through practice one can become better at it.

All the projects made through The Odin Project are public in my Github profile.
